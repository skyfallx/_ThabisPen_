The cries of a family man

I wake up every day, To hustle for my family. Even though I get no respect, I try to provide for them daily. My hands are a desert, I work for hours without rest. But despite all this, I still receive the worst.
Dusk till dawn, It has been years since I sat down. I'm going through pain, I have no choice but to bear. I am a father and a family man.
My wife's insults, Are all I can hear. From her voice and shouts, I have developed a fear.
My children are embarrassed. 'He's not my father', they say. Insecure, I've become. For them to love me, I PRAY.
